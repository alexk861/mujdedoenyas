Nocturne in C-sharp Minor, Op. posth.

128,402 Views Recorded Oct 14, 2023 favorite 12.4K
The Narrative

There is a specific kind of silence that precedes the opening theme of the C-sharp minor Nocturne. It is not the absence of sound, but rather a heavy, expectant quiet—the kind found in a room where a candle has just been extinguished. This performance was captured at midnight in the studio, where the only light came from the moon reflecting off the lacquer of the instrument.

Technically, the challenge of this piece lies not in the speed of the cadenzas, but in the weight of the *rubato*. Each phrase must breathe naturally, mimicking a human sigh. The trills in the middle section are played with a deliberate crispness, contrasting the somber, lyrical nature of the outer themes.

music_note

Frédéric Chopin

Composed in 1830 for his sister Ludwika as an exercise before starting his second concerto, this piece was only published 26 years after the composer's death. It remains one of Chopin's most poignant expressions of melancholic beauty, often cited as a masterpiece of the Romantic era.

Era

Early Romantic (1830)
